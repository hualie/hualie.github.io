Full Model Reconstruction of Hand-size Objects Using A Structured Light Scanner

We develop a turntable-free registration approach to reconstruct the complete model of an object. Firstly, point clouds captured from different viewpoints are pairwise coarsely registered by feature matching of their corresponding RGB images. Secondly, we use an appropriate function to evaluate the quality of every pairwise coarse registration and construct a pairwise coarse registration graph which uses point clouds as nodes and the evaluation function between them to weight their corresponding edges. Thirdly, an optimal registration tree will be generated by finding the maximum weight spanning tree of the graph and selecting a node as the root to minimize the depth of the tree. Finally, global fine registration is performed by applying the ICP algorithm along the optimal registration tree.
